# How to Write Blog Posts — ECOVALUE Guide

**Based on:** [How Do You Avoid AI Slop in 2026?](https://rankenstein.pro/blog/how-to-avoid-ai-slop-2026) by Daniel Agrici (Rankenstein, 2026/02/24)

This guide defines how we write blog content for epcattestprijs.be. Every blog post must follow these rules to avoid AI slop, earn trust, and rank.

## Why This Matters

- 52% of online articles are AI-generated, but human content earns **5.44x more organic traffic** (Graphite/Samwell.ai, 2025)
- ChatGPT cites human-made articles **82% of the time** — even AI prefers human sources
- Only **14% of content ranking** in Google is AI-generated despite producing 52% of all articles — Google silently filters out ~73% of AI content
- Content suspected as AI-generated suffers **48% drop in trust**, 57% decline in authenticity, 60% reduction in emotional connection (Raptive, 2025)
- Consumer preference for AI content dropped from 60% to **26%** in three years (Sprout Social, 2025)

**Bottom line:** AI slop gets published at scale but doesn't survive Google's ranking systems. Human-led content wins.

## The 7 Rules for Every Blog Post

### 1. Start With Original Research or Personal Data

AI can summarize. It can't run a survey, interview a customer, or measure our own results.

Before writing a single paragraph, ask: **"What do we know from direct experience that no search result could tell the reader?"** That's the opening. That's the hook.

**For ECOVALUE this means:**
- Real numbers from our inspections (e.g., "Van de 120 keuringen in Leuven afgelopen kwartaal, had 68% label D of slechter")
- Specific customer scenarios we've encountered
- Before/after EPC scores from real renovations we've inspected
- Dashboard screenshots, anonymized booking data, regional trends we observe
- Quotes from Ermin or our experts about what they see in the field

**Never start a post with a generic AI-written intro.** Start with something only ECOVALUE knows.

### 2. Write the First Draft From Experience, Then Edit With AI

The biggest mistake is starting with AI. Prompt -> generate -> edit = reads like AI because it is AI. **Flip the order.**

**Our workflow:**
1. Expert (Ermin or team) writes rough notes/bullet points from experience
2. Those notes become the first draft — messy is fine, voice matters more
3. AI cleans up grammar, suggests tighter phrasing, fills factual gaps
4. Human does final edit to restore voice and add personality

The 71.7% human-AI blend that ranks well (Ahrefs, 2025) works this way: **human first, AI second.**

### 3. Add Specific Numbers, Dates, and Named Sources

AI slop loves vague claims: "Studies show...", "Experts agree...", "Research suggests..."

**We name names.** Every major claim needs:
- A named source (e.g., "Volgens VEKA's jaarrapport 2025...")
- A date (e.g., "Sinds 1 januari 2026...")
- A sample size or methodology when possible (e.g., "Op basis van 53 Google reviews...")

**Examples of what we write vs. what AI slop writes:**

| AI Slop | ECOVALUE Style |
|---------|---------------|
| "Studies tonen aan dat isolatie de EPC waarde verbetert" | "Dakisolatie verlaagt de EPC score gemiddeld met 80-120 kWh/m2 volgens de VEKA berekenmethode (EPC3, 2023)" |
| "Veel huizen in Vlaanderen scoren slecht" | "62% van de eengezinswoningen in Vlaanderen heeft label D of slechter (Vlaams Energie- en Klimaatagentschap, 2025)" |
| "De premies zijn aanzienlijk" | "De EPC labelpremie bedraagt tot EUR 7.000 voor een eengezinswoning die label A bereikt (MijnVerbouwPremie, 2026)" |

This makes our content citable by both humans and AI systems.

### 4. Break the Pattern: Vary Sentence Lengths and Use Contractions

AI writes in a metronomic rhythm. Every sentence is roughly the same length. It avoids contractions.

**Our style rules:**
- Mix short punches (5-8 words) with longer explanations (20-30 words)
- Use contractions naturally: "isn't" not "is not", "u hoeft niet" not "u hoeft het niet te doen"
- Start sentences with "En", "Maar", "Want" when it flows better
- Write like you'd explain it to a homeowner at their kitchen table
- Avoid these AI-signature phrases:
  - "Het is vermeldenswaard dat..." (it's worth noting)
  - "In het kader van..." (in the realm of)
  - "Concluderend kunnen we stellen..." (in conclusion)
  - "Een breed scala aan..." (a wide range of)
  - "Het is belangrijk op te merken..." (it is important to note)
  - Any sentence starting with "Uiteraard" or "Natuurlijk"

**Test:** Read your paragraph aloud. If it sounds like a Wikipedia article, rewrite it.

### 5. Include Images, Charts, and Media AI Can't Generate

Original visuals beat stock photos every time. A screenshot of a real EPC attest with annotations has more credibility than any generic energy illustration.

**What to include in ECOVALUE blog posts:**
- Screenshots of real (anonymized) EPC attesten with our annotations
- SVG/HTML charts built from real data (we already do this on city pages)
- Photos from actual inspections (with client permission)
- Before/after comparison visuals
- Decision trees and flowcharts (like the beslisboom on /epc-verplicht)
- Tables with real numbers (premies, scores, prices)

**What to avoid:**
- Generic stock photos of houses or solar panels
- AI-generated images (readers recognize them)
- Decorative images that add no information

Every image needs a descriptive alt text for SEO and accessibility.

### 6. Run Your Draft Through an AI Detector as a Quality Signal

Not to "pass" a test — but as an editing compass. If your writing triggers AI detection, your prose has slipped into generic patterns.

**Quick self-check before publishing:**
- [ ] Does any paragraph start the same way as the previous one?
- [ ] Are all sentences roughly the same length?
- [ ] Did you use any of the banned AI-signature phrases from Rule 4?
- [ ] Could this paragraph have been written by someone who never did an EPC inspection?
- [ ] Is there a specific number, date, or named source in every section?

If you answer "yes" to any of these, rewrite that section.

### 7. Update Published Content Every 30-60 Days

AI slop is "publish and forget." It never gets touched again. We update.

**Update checklist:**
- Add new statistics when available (VEKA reports, premie updates, price changes)
- Fix broken external links
- Mention recent changes ("Sinds maart 2026 geldt de nieuwe warmtepomppremie...")
- Update the "Laatst bijgewerkt" date **only when you've made meaningful changes**
- Refresh examples and screenshots
- Add insights from recent inspections

This signals to both Google and readers that a real person maintains this resource.

## Blog Post Structure Template

Every ECOVALUE blog post should follow this structure:

```
## Title (H1)
- Question-based preferred: "Hoe...", "Wat...", "Wanneer..."
- Include primary keyword
- Under 65 characters

## Summary Block (first 130-170 words)
- Dense with specific data points
- Answers the title question immediately
- Citable by AI systems

## Kernpunten (key takeaways)
- 3-5 bullet points with specific numbers
- Scannable for readers who won't read the full article

## Main Content Sections (H2s)
- Question-based headings
- Each section 200-400 words
- Each section contains at least one: specific number, named source, or personal insight
- Internal links to relevant ECOVALUE pages

## Practical Section (tips, checklist, how-to)
- Actionable advice based on our experience
- Real examples from our inspections

## Veelgestelde Vragen (FAQ)
- 3-5 questions with 134-167 word answers (AI citation sweet spot)
- Add FAQPage schema

## CTA
- Link to /epc-attest-aanvragen
- Mention pricing (vanaf EUR 90) and speed (attest binnen 24 uur)
```

## Content Quality Checklist

Run this before publishing any blog post:

### Originality
- [ ] Contains at least one data point only ECOVALUE could know
- [ ] Includes a personal experience or real inspection example
- [ ] Has an opinion or recommendation (not just neutral information)

### Sourcing
- [ ] Every major claim has a named source with date
- [ ] External links go to authoritative sites (vlaanderen.be, VEKA, Livios)
- [ ] Statistics include sample size or methodology when available

### Voice
- [ ] Reads like a human expert, not a Wikipedia article
- [ ] Uses contractions naturally
- [ ] Varies sentence lengths (mix short and long)
- [ ] No AI-signature phrases
- [ ] Consistent "u" form (professional tone)

### SEO
- [ ] Title under 65 characters with primary keyword
- [ ] Meta description 120-160 characters with value proposition
- [ ] Exactly 1 H1, logical H2/H3 hierarchy
- [ ] Summary block in first 170 words
- [ ] 3+ internal links to other ECOVALUE pages
- [ ] All images have descriptive alt text
- [ ] FAQPage schema with 3-5 Q&As

### Technical
- [ ] Article schema with image, author, datePublished, dateModified
- [ ] BreadcrumbList schema
- [ ] Added to sitemap.ts
- [ ] Added to Navbar/Footer navigation
- [ ] Added to llms.txt
- [ ] "Laatst bijgewerkt" date visible on page

### Freshness
- [ ] Calendar reminder set for 30-day update
- [ ] Sources and statistics are from current year when possible

## What NOT to Do

1. **Never publish a pure AI draft** — always add personal experience and original data
2. **Never use generic stock photos** — use real screenshots, charts, or nothing
3. **Never make unsourced claims** — name the source or don't include the stat
4. **Never "publish and forget"** — every post gets updated within 30-60 days
5. **Never start with AI** — write from experience first, use AI to polish
6. **Never copy competitor content structure** — our advantage is being a direct service provider with real inspection data
7. **Never use em dashes (—)** — AI content detection signal (already removed site-wide)
